<p><p>The digital vacuum gauge market is primarily divided into two types: analog and digital gauges. Analog gauges utilize a dial and needle mechanism to display vacuum levels, providing a simple, easy-to-read format but often lacking precision. Digital gauges, on the other hand, utilize electronic sensors to provide precise, real-time measurements of vacuum levels displayed on a digital screen. The digital variety is favored for its accuracy, ease of use, and advanced features like data logging, making it increasingly popular in various applications.</p></p>
