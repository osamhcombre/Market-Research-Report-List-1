<p><p>Particle size analyzing equipment encompasses various techniques to measure particle sizes in diverse applications. Laser diffraction measures particle size distribution by analyzing scattered light patterns. Dynamic light scattering determines particle size in colloids by measuring fluctuations in light intensity. Imaging analysis captures visual data for size determination using microscopy. The Coulter principle counts and sizes particles based on electrical resistance changes. Nanoparticle tracking analysis utilizes light scattering to track individual particles in suspension. Other methods might include sieving and sedimentation techniques.</p></p>
