<p><ul><li>X-Ray</li><li>Infrared Ray</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-contact thickness gauges utilize various technologies to measure material thickness without direct contact. X-ray thickness gauges employ ionizing radiation to determine thickness through absorption levels, offering high precision for dense materials. Infrared ray gauges use infrared light to measure thickness based on reflectance or transmission, ideal for non-metallic materials. Other types may include laser thickness gauges that utilize laser triangulation, or ultrasonic methods that employ sound waves, each catering to specific material properties and industry requirements.</p></p>
